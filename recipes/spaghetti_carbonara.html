Spaghetti Carbonara

A classic Roman pasta dish, Spaghetti Carbonara combines creamy egg sauce, crispy pancetta, and sharp Pecorino Romano cheese for a simple yet decadent meal. Quick to prepare, itâ€™s perfect for a satisfying lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic (optional, for flavoring)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Black pepper (freshly ground, to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional)

Steps:

  1. Cook spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. In a large pan, cook pancetta over medium heat until crispy. If desired, add garlic cloves for flavoring, then remove them before proceeding.
  3. In a bowl, whisk eggs with Pecorino Romano and Parmesan cheese. Season with black pepper.
  4. Add cooked spaghetti to the pan with pancetta, tossing to coat in the rendered fat. Remove from heat.
  5. Quickly mix the egg and cheese mixture with the hot pasta, stirring vigorously to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water as needed to adjust consistency.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with additional cheese and black pepper.
